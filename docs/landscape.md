# Landscape

Snapshot based on a research pass completed on 2026-04-26.

## What the market is converging on

Most serious players are moving toward some combination of:

- cross-app action
- persistent context or memory
- ambient AI surfaces
- policy-bounded autonomy

## Closest incumbents

### Google / Android

Google is the strongest incumbent on deep Android integration through Gemini and broader Android AI surfaces. It is closest to the "AI woven into the OS" direction, but the control plane is still Google-owned rather than user-owned.

### Apple

Apple is building toward deeper system integration through Apple Intelligence, App Intents, and future Siri capabilities like personal context understanding and in-app action. Strong system position, closed platform, slower rollout.

### Samsung

Samsung is especially relevant because it is moving toward a multi-agent model, with Galaxy AI positioned as a broader orchestration surface rather than a single assistant brand.

### Perplexity

Perplexity is becoming a real mobile layer, not just a search app. Its Android assistant and deeper OEM partnerships suggest a serious attempt to become the "AI control surface" on top of existing phones.

## OEMs and adjacent players

### Lenovo / Motorola Qira

Qira is conceptually close to a persistent personal operator: ambient, contextual, and action-oriented across devices.

### Deutsche Telekom

Telekom is notable for trying to package agentic behavior for mainstream users rather than only builders or power users.

### Nothing

Nothing's Essential Space is a strong wedge around memory and action, even if it is not a full operator.

### HONOR and OPPO

Both are publicly pointing toward agentic or AI-driven OS experiences, including GUI-level agents and user knowledge systems.

### Rabbit

Rabbit remains important as a signal that people want app abstraction and teachable agents, even if the implementation path differs from a true mobile OS.

## Open-source and hacker energy

There is visible energy around:

- Android-native assistants
- persistent personal agents
- OpenClaw-like mobile ports

But there is still no clear winner at the open-source, OS-native, user-controlled personal operator layer.

## Working conclusion

The assistant layer is crowded.

The open, user-owned, AOSP-native operator layer is still open territory.
