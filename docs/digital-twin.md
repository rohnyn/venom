# Digital Twin

## Working definition

The digital twin is not an LLM pretending to be the user.

It is a structured operating construct made of:

- durable user memory
- a delegation envelope
- a policy profile
- a task and action history
- a preference and routine model

## Why the idea is powerful

If the phone has a persistent model of the user, it can move from one-shot assistance to representation in bounded domains.

Examples:

- triaging notifications
- drafting responses
- booking or scheduling under policy
- preparing routine workflows before the user asks

## Why the idea is risky

"Digital twin" easily turns into overclaiming or creepiness if it implies:

- full identity substitution
- social action without boundaries
- unclear consent
- undeclared background surveillance

## Safe interpretation

The twin should be:

- personal
- inspectable
- bounded by user policy
- interruptible
- progressively delegated rather than fully trusted at the start

## Working open question

Whether "digital twin" should remain an internal architecture term or become part of the external product story.
