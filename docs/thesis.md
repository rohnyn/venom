# Thesis

## Problem

Phones are the primary personal compute surface, but current mobile AI is still shallow:

- trapped inside apps
- weak memory
- limited system access
- poor extensibility
- little durable delegation

## Why mobile matters

Mobile has the richest stream of personal context:

- messages
- notifications
- location
- camera and mic
- routines
- payment and commerce intent
- social and communication workflows

If a persistent operator is going to matter anywhere, it should matter on the phone.

## Why now

Three things are converging:

- models are becoming good enough to support bounded autonomy
- OEMs and platform owners are moving toward agentic phone experiences
- users are already acclimating to assistive AI in search, messaging, summarization, and automation

## Core design line

Build tomorrow's substrate under today's UX.

That means:

- keep familiar mobile surfaces
- add a persistent operator underneath them
- increase delegated authority over time as the models improve

## End-state

The primary abstraction becomes:

- a user-owned operator with memory
- a policy engine that governs it
- a capability layer that lets it act across the device

Apps remain important, but increasingly as execution environments rather than the only way users express intent.
